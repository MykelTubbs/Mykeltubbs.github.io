History

The Wide Receiver is one of the most important positions on the football field. With responsibilites of blocking, catching and scoring touchdowns, the wide reciever position has been important since the creation of the game. The name "wide receiver" come from the literal postioning on the football field. Receivers are out wide from the formation of the offense , so the term was created. There can be multiple receivers on the field at one time . Depending on the offense there can be 2, 3, or 4 receivers on the field at one time.

Before the 1930's receiver were attached to the end of the offensive line and were defined as "ends" because during this age , the forward pass wasnt allowed. Don Hutson is considered the first modern wide receiver. He played for Alabama in college and the Green Bay Packers in the NFL.

Notable Players

Jerry Rice is widely knwon as the greatest receiver of all time. For his career he gained over 22 thousand yards and almost 200 touchdowns. This hall of fame receiver has served as a blueprint for many receivers who have come after him but no one has surpassed him yet.
